Nasi Lemak

Nasi-Lemak

Description

Nasi Lemak is a traditional dish from Malaysia. It consists of peanuts, cucumber, half boiled egg, fried anchovies and sambal, a spicy type of sauce. As an additional option, fried chicken and squid can also be added.

Different shops has their own version of Nasi Lemak. For example, there are spicy and also sweet type of sambal depending on their own recipes. One of the factors that differentiates delicious Nasi Lemak is usually the fried chicken. Malaysians prefer freshly fried and juicy chicken to be eaten with their Nasi Lemak.

Ingredients

  • Anchovies
  • Sambal
  • Peanuts
  • Cucumber
  • Half boil eggs
  • Coconut rice
  • Fried chicken

Steps

  1. Prepare the coconut rice by cooking it with coconut milk and pandan leaves. Add ginger for a full burst of flavor.
  2. To cook sambal, add lots of shallots, ginger, dry shrimp paste and blended chillies into a wok an d mix until everything is mixed together well. The rule here is the longer you cook it, the more tastier your sambal will get.
  3. For fried chicken, add curry leaves and tumeric powder. Marinate it for a few hours in a fridge and fry it!
  4. Fry your anchovies until golden brown and put it aside for a few minutes.
  5. Plate your nasi lemak as shown in the picture. If you have banana leaves around, put it on a plate to give it a traditional vibe.
